OUTER-SPACE DESCRIPTION

    What is outer space?

    This is the vast expanse beyond Earth's atmosphere, where celestial bodies like planets, stars and gallaxies reside.

    It is a near-perfect vacuum, meaning there's very little matter present.

SHOWCASE

THE SUN

Massive: The sun is enormous, containing 99.86% 0f the total mass of our solar system.

Nuclear Fusion: The sun's energy comes from nuclear fusion, where hydrogen atoms combine to form helium, releasing immense amount of energy.

Heat and Light: This energy is emitted as heat and light, essential for life on Earth.

LIfe: The sun provides the heat and light necessary for life on Earth.

THE EARTH

Earth is the third planet from the sun and the only planet known to support life.

Liquid water: The presence of abudant liquid water is crucial for life as we know it.

Atmosphere: Earth has a protective atmosphere containing oxygen, essential for many life forms.

Plate Tectonics The earth's surface is divided into plates that slowly move, shaping continents and causing earthquakes and volcanoes.

JUPITER

Jupiter, the fifth planet from the sun, is a gas giant known for its immense size and striking appearance.

Largest planet: Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with a diameter about 11 times that of Earth.

Gas Giant: It's primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, lacking a solid surface.

Jupiter has a system of faint less prominent than Saturn's.

NEPTUNE

Ice Giant: Neptune is classified as an "Ice giant" due to its composition, which is primarily a slushy mix of water, methane and ammonia over a solid, rocky core.

Extreme Winds: Neptune boasts the strongest winds in the solar system. reaching speeds of over 1,200 miles per hour (2000 kilometers per hour).

MERCURY

Closest to the sun: Mercury orbits the sun at an average distance of about 57.9 million kilometers (35.9 million miles), making it th closest planet to our star.

Small planet: Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system, with a diameter of only about 4,880 kilometers(3030 miles).It's even smaller then some of the moons in our solar system, like Jupiter's Ganymede and Saturn's Titan.

VENUS

Venus often known as Earth's Twin due to their similarity in size and structure, is a fascinating planet with some unique characteristics.

Hottest Planet: Despite being farther from the sun than Mercury, Venus boasts the hottest surface temperature in our solar system reaching a scorching 867 degrees Fahrenheit.This extreme heat is due to a runway greenhouse effect caused by its thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide.
